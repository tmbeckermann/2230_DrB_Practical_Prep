Bones and Markings Checklist

What this isThe course checklist of lower-limb bones and markings students may see labeled on physical models.
What to knowBe able to answer a sticker-style prompt such as "What is the marking labeled 2B?" and identify left versus right when relevant.
How to use itFilter by region, mark items as done, use Visuals to stretch limited lab time, and confirm with physical models in lab.

Common traps

  • Condyle and epicondyle are not interchangeable; epicondyles sit above condyles.
  • Intertrochanteric line is anterior; intertrochanteric crest is posterior.
  • Medial malleolus belongs to tibia; lateral malleolus belongs to fibula.
  • The fibular apex, or styloid process, is posterolateral on the fibular head.
  • For L/R practice, identify the view first, then decide side.

Muscles to ID

Use this to match model tags to muscle names.
What this isThe lower-limb muscle identification list tied to practical model tags and images when available.
What to knowIdentify the tagged muscle on a physical model, including side or view when that changes the answer.
How to use itPractice from the tag number first, then use linked Visuals or lab images before checking the model in lab.

Students are expected to identify these muscles on physical lab models; this page is a guide for practice when lab time is limited.
